Word stress: in Greek-origin Academic Words

Word stress: English syllables are weak or strong. The weak sounds you can’t hear clearly; the strong sounds must be clear to understand the word. Listen and repeat these words, and then the words in sentences.

biology = bi-O-lo-gy
biologist = bi-O-lo-gist
biological = bi-o-LO-gi-cal

Biology is an essential part of the high school curriculum.

The biologist have discovered thousands of new species.

Scientists are conducting biological research to better understand how ecosystems are affected by climate change.

Notice: the E-co-systems stress (like many Greek-origin academic words) is third from the end.
psychology = psy-CHO-lo-gy
psychologist = psy-CHO-lo-gist
psychological = psy-cho-LO-gi-cal

Psychology helps us understand the complexities of human behavior and mental processes.

A skilled psychologist can help individuals navigate challenges.

The study revealed significant psychological effects on individuals experiencing prolonged stress.

Notice: Greek spelling/pronunciation: psy = “sigh”, ch = “k”.
So the words sound like: sy-KO-lojy · sy-KO-lo-jist · sy-ko-LOJ-i-cal

Listen again.
